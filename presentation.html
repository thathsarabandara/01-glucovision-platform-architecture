AI-Powered Solutions for Diabetes Management

A Systematic Review of Emerging Technologies

Research Period: 2014-2025 | 38 Studies Analyzed

Focus: Sri Lanka & Similar Contexts

The Problem: Diabetes in Sri Lanka

3.6%

of Sri Lankans (18-29 years) have diabetes

23%

of adults (18+) affected

55%

prevalence in ages 60-69

Key Challenge: Current systems don't account for culturally diverse eating habits and traditional Sri Lankan cuisine

Research Scope: Six Key Technologies

  • 🍽️ Food Recognition & Classification - AI identifying dishes from images
  • 📏 Portion Estimation - Determining serving sizes accurately
  • ⌚ Wearable Sensor Monitoring - Continuous health tracking
  • 📊 Glucose Prediction - Forecasting blood sugar levels
  • 👤 Digital Twin Technology - Virtual patient models
  • 🔐 Federated Learning - Privacy-preserving AI

Food Recognition Systems

Advanced Deep Learning Approaches

  • ResNet & Vision Transformers: Direct prediction of dish mass and nutrients
  • RGB-D Methods: Mask R-CNN achieves 94.1% detection accuracy
  • Performance Metrics: 91-93% classification accuracy on international datasets
⚠️ Critical Gap: Limited recognition of regional Sri Lankan dishes

Portion Estimation

Wearable Sensors & Continuous Monitoring

Real-Time Health Tracking

  • Data Collected: Physical activity, heart rate, glucose levels, energy expenditure
  • Behavioral Integration: Pattern analysis for better insights
  • Key Advantage: Non-invasive, suitable for resource-limited settings
  • Practical Application: Enables busy populations to monitor health continuously
Benefit: Especially helpful for people with busy lives where regular hospital-based monitoring is difficult

Glucose Prediction & Analytics

Machine Learning for Prevention

  • Data Source: Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) analysis
  • Performance: Deep learning models outperform linear approaches
  • Early Detection: Identifies hypoglycemic events before occurrence
  • Innovation: Multi-modal wearable sensors for non-invasive prediction
  • Outcome: Individualized care with early intervention capability

Digital Twin Technology

Personalized Virtual Health Models

Concept: Virtual patient combining clinical data + wearable data + lifestyle information

Key Benefits:
  • Forecast future health changes safely
  • Identify unhealthy foods proactively
  • Optimize therapy without patient risk
  • Personalized nutrition planning for Type 2 diabetes

Federated Learning: Privacy-Preserving AI

Collaborative Learning Without Data Exposure

  • How it Works: Models learn from multi-institutional data
  • Privacy Protection: Raw patient data never exposed to central system
  • Healthcare Applications: Glucose forecasting, diabetic predictions across centers
  • Regulatory Benefit: Addresses data privacy constraints and compliance
Impact: Enables large-scale collaborative systems while maintaining data security

Key Findings & Research Gaps

What Works ✅

  • AI achieves human-level accuracy for food recognition
  • Wearable sensors enable continuous monitoring
  • Predictive models show strong promise for glucose control

Critical Gaps ❌

  • Limited recognition of South Asian/Sri Lankan cuisine
  • Most systems tested in controlled environments only
  • Underrepresentation of cultural eating patterns
  • No integrated end-to-end system combining all technologies

Future Recommendations for Sri Lanka

1. Build Cultural Datasets 📚

Sri Lankan food recognition models & regional dietary patterns

2. Accessible Technology 📱

Portable AR glasses vs. expensive equipment; smartphone-based solutions

3. Integrated Platform 🔗

Combine all technologies in one solution with privacy-first design

4. Community-Centric Design 👥

Consider literacy levels & align with local healthcare systems

Conclusion: The Future of Diabetes Management
